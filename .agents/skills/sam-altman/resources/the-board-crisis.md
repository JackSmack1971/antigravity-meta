# sam-altman: The Board Crisis (November 2023)

## Timeline

- **November 17, 2023:** Sudden termination by the OpenAI board (Adam D'Angelo, Tasha McCauley, Helen Toner, Ilya Sutskever). No specific reason given. Greg Brockman resigns in solidarity.

- **November 18-20, 2023:** Microsoft offers Sam and Greg a new lab with "unlimited" resources. Emmett Shear is named interim CEO.

- **November 20, 2023:** Over 700 of 770 OpenAI employees sign a letter threatening to quit if the board doesn't resign and Sam is not reinstated.

- **November 22, 2023:** Sam returns as CEO with a reconstructed board (Bret Taylor, Larry Summers). Adam D'Angelo remains for continuity.

## Ilya Sutskever’s Role

- **Action:** Voted for the split, but then expressed deep regret and publicly supported Sam's return.

- **Reasoning:** Driven by a spiritual and technical concern for AGI Safety.

- **Result:** While reconciled, the operational trust was altered. Ilya left in May 2024 to found SSI (Safe Superintelligence).

## Core Lessons

- **Organizational Power:** Real power in mission-driven companies doesn't come from the org chart; it comes from collective belief in the mission.

- **Trust Capital:** The overwhelming employee support was a direct result of yearlong transparency and directness.

- **Governance Stress Test:** Proved that traditional board structures were insufficient for overseeing the development of AGI.

## Microsoft and Satya Nadella

- **Partnership:** Satya’s immediate support revealed a rare level of corporate loyalty and alignment.

- **Autonomy:** Microsoft’s willingness to allow OpenAI to operate as an independent lab remains its greatest strength.
