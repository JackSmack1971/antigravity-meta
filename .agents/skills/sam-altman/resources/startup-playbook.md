# sam-altman: The Startup Playbook (YC Wisdom)

## Make Something People Want
The foundational mantra. "Want" means people would be **devastated** if the product disappeared. FRUSTRATED is not enough.

## Market Identification

- **Growth > Size:** A small, fast-growing market is better than a large, stagnant one.

- **Real Pain:** Solve problems people already spend money and time on.

- **Unobvious Advantage:** Ensure the business is defensible through more than just capital.

- **Founder-Market Fit:** Solve problems you have personally experienced.

## Co-Founders

- **Complementary Skills:** Tech vs. Biz.

- **Relationship:** Historical experience working under pressure.

- **Failed Startups:** Most fail due to founder conflict, not product-market fit.

## Hiring and Firing

- **Hire Slow:** Each of the first 20 employees defines the culture.

- **Learning Velocity:** Hire for how fast someone learns, not just what they know.

- **Fire Fast:** Keeping non-performing team members signals a low bar and drives away top talent.

## Fundraising (SAFE Notes)

- **SAFE Notes:** Simple Agreement for Future Equity. Solves early-stage negotiation friction.

- **Valuation ≠ Validation:** High early valuations are a liability that creates an impossible bar for future rounds.

- **Raising Capital:** Raise the minimum needed to reach the next clear milestone. Cash excess leads to premature scaling and culture rot.

- **Investor Selection:** Choose investors for long-term partnership (7-10 years), not just capital.

## Pivot vs. Persevere

- **Pivot:** If growth is flat after 12-18 months of effort, or if a niche segment loves the product far more than the general intended audience.

- **Persevere:** If there is consistent growth and high retention, even with a small user base.
