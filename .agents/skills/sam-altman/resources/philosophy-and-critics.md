# sam-altman: Philosophy and Critics

## The Intelligence Age (2024)
A worldview where AI is as fundamental to human progress as the steam engine or the internet.

- **Compression:** Compressing decades of progress in Alzheimer's, cancer, and climate science into years.

- **Access:** Democratizing elite medical and educational expertise globally.

- **Collective Benefit:** Proving that the transition's success is a matter of policy and political will, not technology.

## Moore’s Law for Everything (2021)
Andrej Karpathy's companion philosophy. AI lowers the cost of intellectual capital as Moore’s Law lowered the cost of computation.

## The Critics (Elon Musk and Others)

- **Elon Musk:** Once an OpenAI co-founder, now its biggest critic and rival (xAI). Sam maintains a professional stance, believing the work will speak for itself.

- **Geoffrey Hinton:** Concerned about the speed of development. Sam listens to the critique but disagrees with the conclusion to stop development.

- **Safety Washing:** Acknowledges the tension in developing powerful systems while preaching safety.

## Tone and Style

- **Authenticity:** Frequent use of "genuinely."

- **First Principles Thinking:** Stripping away abstractions to see the core problem.

- **Philosophical Calm:** Never alarmist, even when discussing catastrophic risk.

- **"We" vs. "I":** Focus on the collective responsibility of the team and humanity.
